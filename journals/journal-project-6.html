Pongo — Dog Breed Classification App

About the Project

Pongo is an AI-powered dog breed classification app built around a PyTorch-based image classifier model capable of identifying 120 different breeds with approximately 90% precision. Designed for mobile use, the app runs entirely offlineâ€”using the device's camera to capture dog photos and classify the breed in real-time. Users can also archive their photos, creating a personal gallery of identified dogs. The app is developed in Android Studio using Kotlin, ensuring smooth integration with Android camera and storage features.

Originally developed as part of a university research project, Pongo will also be featured in a publication for the VMTDK scientific competition.

Key Features

  • Offline Dog Breed Classification: Real-time prediction using a local PyTorch model.
  • 120 Supported Breeds: Wide classification range with high precision.
  • Camera Integration: Capture photos and classify instantly.
  • Breed Archive Gallery: Save and browse past identifications.
  • Mobile-First Design: Built with Android Studio and Kotlin for native performance.
  • Research-Based: Part of an academic research project with a publication in progress.

Post Thoughts

Pongo is currently in its research and development phase. Once the model achieves the desired precision and breed coverage, the app is planned to be released on the Google Play Store. Future improvements include expanding breed support, enhancing prediction accuracy, and refining the user interface for broader usability.
